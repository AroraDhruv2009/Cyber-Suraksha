Types Of Cyber Security Threats -



Phishing


Phishing is a type of scam where the scammers disguise as a trustworthy source in attemp to obtain private information such as passwords, and credit card information, stc. through the internet. These fake websites are often designed to look identical to their legitimate counterparts to avoid suspicion from the user.



Man in the Middle


Man-in-the-middle (MitM) attacks, also known as eavesdropping attacks, occur when attackers insert themselves into a two-party transaction, Once the attackers interrupt the traffic, they can filter and steal data. For example, once malware has breached a device, an attacker can install apps to process all of the victim's information.



Malware


Malware is one of the most prevalent security concerns as often it is impossible to determine whether a file is infected, despite the source of the file. Malware, particularly spyware, is malicious software disguised as software designed to collect and transmit private information, such as password, without the user's consent or knowledge.
